Common Mistakes

Why names fail.

Most naming projects collapse before they begin. Not from lack of creativity — but from a lack of linguistic discipline. Here are the four patterns I see most often.

01

Phonetic friction

If a customer can't spell it after hearing it once, you're paying an acquisition tax on every word-of-mouth referral — forever.

Highest impact failure
02

Describing, not evoking

Names like "FastPay" or "SmartHealth" describe a feature, not an identity. They date quickly and are nearly impossible to trademark.

Most common mistake
03

Naming by committee

When everyone has a vote, the safest name wins. Safe names are forgettable names. Great naming requires a single point of strategic conviction.

Process failure
04

Chasing the domain

Letting domain availability dictate the name is like choosing a building's architecture based on the nearest parking space. The name comes first.

Strategic failure
